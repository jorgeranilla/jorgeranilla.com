Ancestry

Our family's journey from the mountains and coasts of Peru to Lima. A story of migration, perseverance, and the values that shaped generations.

Our Peruvian Roots

Our family's story is one of migration, hard work, and determination. From humble beginnings in different regions of Peru, our grandparents migrated to Lima, where through education and sacrifice, they built a better future for generations to come.

The Journey from Arequipa

On my father's side, Abuela María Jesús Cateriano Dongo was born in the agricultural valley of Majes, Arequipa. She brought the strength and resilience of Arequipa to our family. Abuelo Sergio Raúl Ranilla Rondon came from the coastal town of Camaná, Arequipa, carrying with him the determination and honor that would define our family values.

Both made the journey to Lima, seeking better opportunities and a brighter future for their children.

From the Mountains and Highlands

On my mother's side, Mamá Giya (Alcira Victoria López Ruiz) came from the lush mountains of Rodríguez de Mendoza, Amazonas. She brought warmth, wisdom, and the spirit of the jungle to our family. Papaqueno (Eugenio Astocondor Salazar) descended from the highlands of Canta, Lima to the capital, bringing strength and dedication that shaped our family.

Their migration to Lima was driven by the same hope: to provide education and opportunities for the next generation.

Our Generation

Thanks to the sacrifice and hard work of our grandparents, our entire generation was born and raised in Lima. Though we are Limeños, we carry with us the heritage of Arequipa, Amazonas, and Canta. We remember where we came from and honor the journey that brought us here.
